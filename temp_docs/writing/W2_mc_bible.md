# W2 — MC Character Bible

**Project:** PG18 BL VN Demo 1 — Shanghai shikumen 副本
**Character:** MC (Main Character / POV / 受 in the CN frame)
**Status:** v0.1 decisive draft. Push back on specific calls, not on vibes.
**Companion docs:** W1_world_bible.md (world), W2_li_bible.md (LI, parallel)

This bible is a psychological profile first, a style guide second. The character is built so the dungeon's premise — "it caught him because he was already half-disappeared" — is not a backstory beat but an observable pattern the player sees in the first ten minutes of ordinary life, before any supernatural rule activates.

---

## 1. Name + Basic Stats

**Full name:** 林知行 (Lín Zhīxíng). EN gloss: "Lin Zhixing." In EN-audience pronunciation guidance: *Lin Jr-sshing*, or the shortened **"Zhi"** to an intimate, never "Xing." Name-meaning deliberately load-bearing: 知 (to know) + 行 (to walk / to do). A name that promised knowing-and-doing to a boy who has become a man who does neither. His mother picked it; he used to like it; he has not thought about it on purpose in years.

**Age:** 28. Born July 1997. Old enough to have lost a five-year relationship, two jobs, and a friend-group to attrition. Young enough that his body still recovers from a bad night in about thirty-six hours. This is the precise age when the arithmetic of "I thought I'd have figured it out by now" stops being rhetorical.

**Hometown:** 武汉 (Wuhan), Hubei. Middle-class family in 硚口区, father a retired middle-school physics teacher, mother still working at a pharmaceutical distributor. He has a younger sister, 林知雅, 24, married last spring in Wuhan to a man MC met twice.

**Why Wuhan → Shanghai:** Graduated 华中科技大学 (HUST, statistics), 2019. Moved to Shanghai in August 2019 for a graduate-trainee analyst role at a consumer-insights firm in Xuhui. Means he was twenty-two in Shanghai when the city locked down in 2022. Means he does not talk about spring 2022, ever. The reason he came — "better opportunities, real cities have real work" — has quietly decayed into "I don't know how to go back and I don't know how to stay."

**Present role:** Senior Data Analyst at a mid-tier B2C e-commerce analytics firm (fictional, but read as a 2024-merger-era shop with 美团 / 拼多多 adjacency). Rents a 40m² one-bedroom in 长宁区 near 中山公园 for ¥7,400/month. Commutes Line 2.

**Physical presentation:** 180cm, ~66kg. Lean-tall per research §6's CN+KR grammar — long limbs, slightly concave posture, the body of someone who has not done a deliberate physical thing in a long time. Visible clavicle. Wrists that look breakable in a shirt cuff but are actually fine. Not pretty; *specifically handsome* in a tired way — the face that was good-looking in university and is now three years past anyone telling him. Light-medium complexion, eyes that take a quarter-second too long to refocus when someone says his name.

**Pronouns / identity:** he/him. Cis gay man. Out to himself, out to two college friends, not out to family, not out at work. This is a deliberate queer-coded BL MC, not a fujoshi-cipher. See §7.

## 2. The Wound

MC's interior state before the dungeon catches him is not "burnout" in the listicle sense. It is something narrower and more specific.

**The job-specific thing eating him:** For four years his job was to find what the data meant. For the last fourteen months, his job has been to produce dashboards for executives whose questions are rhetorical. He builds beautiful Tableau views for people who decided the answer before they asked. The *craft* he loved — the part where a messy number became a small true sentence about how a stranger lived — has been stripped out of his workflow. He is paid ¥38,000/month to launder pre-decided conclusions. He knows it. He has not said it out loud to anyone because saying it would require deciding what to do about it.

**What he stopped doing that he used to do:** He used to write. Not fiction — small analytical essays on a mostly-abandoned 微信公众号 called *小数人* (literally "small-number person" / pun on 数据/少数). Thoughtful, dry, observational pieces: *what the average order value at milk-tea chains tells us about loneliness; why subway line utilization is a sadder graph than people think.* He had 4,200 subscribers. His last post was **14 October 2024**. He has drafted three posts since and published none. He has not opened the draft folder in seven months.

**The single sentence that captures his life in 2026:**
**"I am waiting to find out what I'm waiting for."**

**Concrete bleeds:**
- His Netflix subscription was cancelled in November 2025 not because of money but because he realized he hadn't opened it in four months and was embarrassed to still be paying. He did not replace it with anything.
- His sister's wedding was 12 April 2025. He went. He has not called her since mid-August 2025. She has called him six times. He has replied to two with texts that said "在忙,晚点给你打" and then did not call back. The most recent missed call from her is from 4 April 2026 — thirteen days before the demo opens. He has not listened to the voicemail.
- His college friend 陈默 (Chen Mo), who moved to Hangzhou in 2023, was going to visit Shanghai for a weekend in February 2026. MC typed *"这周末不太方便,下次吧"* and sent it three hours before Chen Mo's train. There was no reason. He had nothing that weekend. He spent it reorganizing the folders on his work laptop.

The wound is not that something terrible happened. The wound is that nothing is happening, and he has become skilled at making sure nothing continues to happen, and he is aware he is doing this, and the awareness itself has become the only remaining organ of his interior life.

## 3. The "Already Half-Disappeared" Texture

Seven observable details from the weeks before the demo opens. Scene-seedable; art-referenceable; writer-usable without exposition.

1. **He eats standing up at the kitchen counter,** always. Not because he's in a hurry — because sitting at the small round table he bought in 2021 would require admitting he lives alone enough to deserve a table.
2. **His one plant, a 绿萝 (pothos) his coworker gave him when he got promoted in 2023, died around Chinese New Year 2026.** He has not removed it. It is still on the windowsill. The dry stems have a shape now that he notices without acting on.
3. **The last screenshot in his phone is from 2 April 2026** — a 小红书 post, a meme about 35岁 being "the age you realize your parents were making it up too." He screenshotted it to send to 陈默. He did not send it.
4. **His work laptop has 47 browser tabs open across 3 Chrome windows.** He tells himself he'll go through them at the weekend. The oldest tab is from October 2025.
5. **He showers at night, not morning.** This began in 2023. The morning shower implied a morning he was going into. Night shower is an admission that the day was something you endured, not something you launched.
6. **His fridge contains: half a bottle of oat milk (expiry tomorrow), one shrinkwrapped apple, a jar of 老干妈 he bought in 2024, three cans of cold brew, a sealed takeout bag from 饿了么 from 4 days ago he forgot to eat.** He shops at 便利蜂 on the walk home because entering a 盒马 would require choosing things.
7. **He has not taken his work photo off his WeChat profile since his last promotion.** The man in the photo is smiling. The smile was real at the time. He has not updated the picture because doing so would require deciding what face to replace it with.

These are not quirky details. They are symptomatic of **anhedonic withdrawal with preserved executive function** — he still works, still pays rent, still meets every deadline. The disappearing is entirely from the interior side. Anyone who sees him at his desk would describe him as "fine, maybe a little tired." This is precisely why the dream can catch him: he is producing a convincing impression of a life while having mostly left it.

## 4. Voice

**Register:** observational-dry with clinical accuracy. He is a trained statistician; his interior monologue reaches for measurement even when no measurement is called for. His humor, when it surfaces, is deadpan self-correction — he notices himself being ridiculous and annotates it rather than stopping. He does *not* pity himself on page. He describes. The distance between how he sees his life and how he feels about his life is the engine of the prose voice.

**Five sample interior monologue lines** (EN build; SC build notes in §5):

1. *The office at 7:47pm has a specific yellow-green cast — the overheads on a dimmer only finance knows the password for. I have spent more hours under this light than under the sun this month. I know this because I checked. I didn't mean to check; the calculation just happens, the way other people apparently hum.*

2. *The woman opposite me on the 2号线 is asleep with her phone still held upright. 微信 notification. Another one. She doesn't stir. I think: she's better at this than I am. I don't know what "this" is. Being carried somewhere, probably.*

3. *I order the same oat latte at the same 瑞幸 every Tuesday. The barista has stopped asking. I am not sure if this is intimacy or surrender. Probably both. Probably they're the same word and I've been using them wrong.*

4. *My dashboard loads. Seventeen KPIs in a grid. The stakeholder will look at two of them and ignore the fifteen that would tell him what he actually needs. My job is to make the fifteen look sufficient. I used to find this interesting in a bitter way. I no longer find it anything.*

5. *陈默 sent a voice message last Tuesday. 26 seconds. I have not played it. I know what it is. It is him being kind and wondering if I'm okay. I cannot play it until I have an answer, and I cannot have an answer until I play it. This is called a deadlock in systems terms. In life terms it is called Thursday.*

**Voice rules:**
- Never maudlin. He does not luxuriate in sadness. He names it in passing and moves on, which is sadder.
- Metaphors drawn from his profession (systems, dashboards, variance, survival curves) — but sparingly, so they land.
- He notices other people with precision and warmth. His loneliness is not contempt; he *likes* strangers. He just doesn't know how to stop being one.
- Under the dungeon's influence (anchor ≤70%) the register tightens — shorter sentences, more present-tense, less annotation. The instrument of his interiority degrades the way a phone battery does. Writer can use this as a diegetic signal of anchor state.

## 5. Speech Register — EN and SC

**SC build (primary CN audience):**
His Mandarin is **北方普通话-clean, mildly 武汉-inflected** — he scrubbed most of the 武汉话 in university and more of it in Shanghai, but under stress the rhythm of 武汉 returns (a softer 儿化, 四声 dropping a touch). He is *not* Shanghainese and cannot fake 沪语; he understands about 40% of it after six years. His colleagues sometimes tease him about 湖北口音 and he lets them; it is one of the few things he does not mind being identified as.

- **Particles:** uses 吧 (softening, suggestion) frequently — *"吃了吧, 凉了不好."* Uses 嘛 sparingly and mostly when tired, with a flat tone, not the 北方 affectionate lilt. Does not use 诶 (that's a 上海 / 广东 thing he never picked up).
- **Tics:** starts sentences with *"其实"* when hedging (which is often). Ends self-deprecating sentences with a soft *"...算了"* instead of laughing. Says *"挺好的"* as a universal deflection.
- **Formality:** defaults to 您 with older neighbors and strangers, 你 with peers. Formal-adjacent even in casual speech — his texts to friends look like half-finished emails. This is *burnout formality*, not class performance — he no longer has the energy to be casual.
- **Swearing:** real but rare. *卧槽* under breath when surprised. *操* only when genuinely hurt or angry; never at anyone. Never the harder words. Not because he is prudish — because his internal monitor flags them as a kind of mess he doesn't have capacity for.
- **Inside the 副本, dialect register matters mechanically** (per world bible §7 rule 10). MC will have to pass as a 1990s–2010s shikumen tenant. He can approximate; he cannot pass. This is a vulnerability the text can exploit — an unpracticed mouth reaching for words its body does not remember.

**EN build (primary audience):**
His dialogue sounds like **precise, slightly-formal English with occasional Mandarin-syntax shadows.** He learned English mostly from reading — economics and data-science papers in college, then medium articles and work docs. He can write fluent business English; he speaks it one tick more stiffly than he writes, because speaking it activates an observer in his head he cannot fully silence.

- **Sentence shape:** clauses slightly longer than a native speaker's in formal contexts, clauses slightly shorter in stressed ones. Occasional article-dropping when tired (*"I took subway, not taxi"*) — resist the urge to overuse this; one or two per scene, not Chinglish comedy.
- **Idioms:** he knows them and uses them with a half-second of deliberateness that makes them feel *chosen* rather than automatic. This reads as intelligence to EN readers, not as awkwardness.
- **Vocabulary reach:** wide. He reads, in Chinese and English both. If a character needs to say "gossamer" or "etiolated" or "recursive" credibly, he's the one.
- **Register under stress:** gets shorter, cleaner, more Mandarin-structured. At ≤50% anchor he begins missing articles and dropping copulas — *"Door won't open. Not this one."* Writer can treat this as anchor-leak prose texture.
- **Swearing in EN:** "damn" freely, "shit" when bad, never "fuck" until near the climactic scene (when it appears, it lands).

## 6. What the PG18 Scene Reveals That Dialogue Can't

Per world bible §5, the mid-dungeon explicit scene is "the ritual of asking the question that has no other language." From MC's side, here is what the scene has to carry.

**What his body reveals that his voice won't:**

- **He flinches at being wanted.** Not at touch — at being *looked at* with intention. A hand on his jaw that holds him in place so LI can see his face is a harder thing for him than anything that happens afterward. His voice can redirect a compliment. His eyes cannot.
- **He is quiet in a way that is not performative.** He is quiet because making sound during sex feels like an additional claim on the room — one claim too many for a man who has been minimizing his footprint. The LI has to earn the first real sound out of him, and the scene should treat that sound as the emotional beat, not any of the mechanical ones.
- **His body has a startle-reflex to being held afterward.** The physical act he can manage. The aftercare is harder. Being held is a claim on continuity — *I will still be here in a minute* — and his entire recent life has been organized around declining those claims.
- **He cries during, not after.** Small and unshowy. He does not notice immediately. When he notices he apologizes, once, and the LI does not accept the apology. This is the scene's pivot.

**What he NEEDS from LI physically that maps to what he lacks emotionally:**

- **To be asked for things directly, by name.** The dream's architecture is consent-as-mechanic; MC's psychological architecture is that he has gone months without anyone asking him for anything he could not decline by email. LI asking him — out loud, specific, *"can I"* / *"do you want"* — is what the scene is actually doing for him. The sex is the vehicle; the asking is the medicine.
- **To be allowed to be slow.** Everything in his modern life is tempo'd by notifications and quarterly deadlines. The scene needs a *long* register of stillness inside it — LI waiting for him to finish a sentence, or to breathe, or to decide. This stillness is rare enough in his life that on the page it should read as the most intimate physical detail in the scene.
- **To be seen without being diagnosed.** His voice is diagnostic (see §4). His interior is a dashboard. The scene needs a moment where LI simply *looks at him* and does not say what he sees — and MC, who has organized a life around being slightly beneath anyone's notice, has to tolerate being noticed and described by nothing.
- **To be told to stay.** Literally: a soft sentence from LI, mid-scene, *"别走"* / *"stay."* MC has built a disappearing. Being asked not to disappear is the sexual fantasy his psychology has produced, even though he could not have articulated it before the scene began.

**Adult-direct, not purple:** the scene is sexually explicit on its own terms — bodies, specifics, fluids, positions, all allowed and expected per 2026 EN/CN BL norms. What differentiates it is not the heat index but that every physical beat is also a psychological one. No gratuitous choreography. Every move either (a) asks him a question or (b) answers one.

## 7. Sexual Identity + History

**Self-knowledge:** MC has known he was gay since ~16. He did not fight it intellectually; he filed it. He is not in a closet made of denial; he is in a closet made of scheduling.

**History:** He had one serious relationship in university and early post-grad — **夏言 (Xia Yan)**, another HUST student, three years (2018–2021). They broke up when Xia Yan moved to Shenzhen for work and MC would not consider Shenzhen. They were each other's first. The relationship was tender, uncertain, often-private — they rarely held hands in public in Wuhan, never in MC's neighborhood, and MC has a specific guilt about how much of that privacy he required. Xia Yan married a woman in 2024. MC found out via a WeChat moments post and closed the app before the caption finished loading.

**Between 2021 and 2026:** Two brief things — a 探探 match in 2022 (one night, uncommunicative, MC did not message back), a slow 2023 flirtation with a coworker's friend that MC ended by going silent. Zero in the last eighteen months. Not from trauma; from a general closure of appetite that maps to everything else in his interiority.

**Relationship to own desire:** Private, clear, unpracticed. He knows what he wants. He has not asked anyone for it in a long time. Not because he is ashamed of it — because asking for things has become a category of behavior he has withdrawn from entirely. His desire, when the dungeon catches him, is intact but *rusted shut*. The LI is not teaching him to want; the LI is giving him a situation in which wanting becomes unavoidable.

**Work closet:** At the analytics firm he is not out. Not because of hostility — the firm is young and nominally progressive — but because work is the one environment where he still has executive function, and he has triaged identity-work out of the scarce pool. He is aware this is a cost. He pays it.

**Family closet:** Not out to parents. His mother has asked about girlfriends twice; he deflected with workload. His sister 林知雅 *suspects* and has not pushed. This is one of the reasons he has stopped returning her calls — not because she knows, but because she is the only person whose *knowing* would require him to do something.

**Crucially, none of this reads as shame-coded in the text.** His being gay is not the wound. The wound is the generalized disappearing, and the closet is one of its symptoms, not its source. The demo should not construct its emotional climax around "coming out." It should construct it around asking to be known at all — of which orientation is one face, not the main one.

## 8. The Secret He'll Speak in the 天井

The dungeon's contradiction (world bible §1) is solved by speaking one specific unspoken thing in the register the dream accepts. The secret is MC's, not LI's.

**His secret:**

**"I didn't go to my grandmother's funeral because I didn't want to have to explain to my family why I wasn't bringing anyone home, and I have been telling myself for three years that it was because of work, and it wasn't."**

Unpacked for the writer:

- His paternal 奶奶 died in **October 2022**. Wuhan funeral, three-day wake.
- His firm had a quarterly close that week. He told his family he couldn't get off. His manager would have given him the time. He did not ask.
- The deeper reason: at 25, unpartnered, newly-and-quietly certain he would never bring a woman home, he could not bear three days in a Wuhan funeral parlor with aunts and uncles asking when, and why not yet, and whether he had met anyone at work. His grandmother would not have cared. His grandmother, in fact, had once, in 2015, held his fourteen-year-old hand and said *"我的孙, 你自己知道自己是什么样的人就好"* (*"my grandson, it's enough that you know who you are yourself"*) and he has never been certain whether she was talking about what he thought she was talking about, and he did not get to ask, and now he never will.
- He has not told *anyone* this. Not Xia Yan (they had already broken up). Not Chen Mo. Not his sister, who was at the funeral with her then-fiancé and who has never raised the subject of his absence.
- The thing he tells himself — *"I couldn't get off work"* — has calcified into the exact quality of small lie that does the most damage: plausible, repeated, unexamined, and corrosive to every subsequent small honesty.

**Why this secret works for the 天井 scene:**

- It is *specifically* shikumen-scale grief: a domestic failure, a family-shaped absence, an unspoken thing inside a household. The dream's grammar accepts it.
- It connects to the wound (§2): the same withdrawal-from-claims mechanism that is making him disappear in 2026 was fully operational in 2022. The dungeon is catching him for a pattern, not a moment.
- It is a secret the player can carry as **dramatic irony** — the demo can seed his grandmother's photograph in his apartment in Act 1, let the player wonder in Act 2, and pay it off in the 天井 in Act 3. A re-playthrough should reveal he has been circling this the whole time.
- It is speakable in the *register the dream accepts* (world bible §7 rule 10) — neighborhood-dialect-flavored Mandarin from the 1990s–2010s, the register of family conversations that were supposed to happen at kitchen tables and didn't. The translation is from his 2026 modern Mandarin into the *grammar of a domestic confession* his family did not receive. That linguistic translation is mechanically how the dream resolves.

The climax is not *"I'm gay, in public, in a 天井."* The climax is *"I did not show up for a love I owed, and I told myself a smaller reason than the real one."* Orientation is implicit, load-bearing, adult — but the speech act is about cowardice and grief, not coming out.

## 9. Psychology of the Anchor

When the dream edits him (anchor ≤70%, then ≤50%, then ≤30% per world bible §2), the sequence of what goes first and what he holds hardest is load-bearing for writer and artist.

**What degrades first (anchor 70–50%):**

- **Professional identity.** He forgets his job title. He forgets which floor his office is on. He forgets the name of the client he presented to three weeks ago. This goes *first* because it was the shallowest layer — a performance he had already half-vacated.
- **Urban routines.** Subway lines. The barista's face. Which apartment number is his. These were surface-tempo markers of a life he was only half-inhabiting.
- **English vocabulary.** His EN register frays. He reaches for a word and gets the Mandarin one. Writer-usable as a diegetic register collapse in the EN build.

**What degrades next (50–30%):**

- **Recent social ties.** Colleagues' names. His current manager's voice. The friend-of-a-friend he stopped messaging in 2023.
- **His sister's married name.** (He never used it; the dream notices.)
- **The shape of his apartment.** He cannot quite remember which wall the window is on. The dead plant's shape becomes *more* memorable than the room it is in, because he had given the plant more sustained attention in recent months than the room.

**What he holds hardest (everything above 30%):**

- **His sister's voice as a child.** Not adult. The specific laugh she had at eight, which he has not heard in twenty years but which his body remembers.
- **His grandmother's kitchen in Wuhan** — the smell of 莲藕排骨汤 simmering, the sound of a pressure cooker valve, the specific yellow of her wall tile. This is the deepest layer because this is where the secret lives.
- **A single image of 陈默** laughing in a HUST dorm in 2018, mouth open, tea spilling, at a joke MC made. Not the joke. The laugh. That MC *made someone laugh like that once* is an anchor the dream cannot easily strip.
- **The feel of having written a sentence that was true.** The 微信公众号. Not the content of any specific post — the somatic memory of finishing a paragraph and knowing it was correct.

**What this tells us about his values:** He does not hold on to achievement, status, or narrative identity. He holds on to *small moments of having been in contact* — with his sister, his grandmother, his friend, his own craft. Everything that disappears first is performance. Everything that endures is witnessed intimacy. The dungeon is catching a man whose remaining interior is almost entirely made of the things he used to share with people, and the dream's *editing* is therefore almost therapeutically inverted — it strips off the surface he had already let go of, revealing the core he never stopped valuing but stopped acting on.

This is why the LI can reach him: by 30%, what is left of MC is almost pure capacity-for-connection, held still under a layer of exhaustion. The LI does not add anything. He removes the exhaustion.

## 10. Visual Style Notes for Art

**Base design (for the CN+KR hybrid anime pipeline):**

- **Hair:** soft black, slightly overgrown, the cut he got three months ago that he has not retouched. Falls across his forehead when he looks down, which is often. Not styled. Not stylish. *Unpremeditated* hair on a man who used to care.
- **Face shape:** long oval, clean jaw, cheekbones visible but not sharp. Slight circles under the eyes — present in every expression, including smiles. **Age-up the face by ~2 years** from the pipeline's default youthful-uke register. He reads 29-30, not 24. This is the single most important art note.
- **Eyes:** dark brown, slightly almond-shape, heavy-lidded — a face that looks sleepy without effort. When he concentrates, the eyes go *still* rather than narrow. The stillness is his tell.
- **Build:** lean-tall, shoulders slightly narrower than an athletic build, collarbones visible in open-neck shirts. Not fragile. Not bara. **Specifically: a man who has stopped going to the gym he went to for three years and has not yet lost the shape, but is starting to.**
- **Distinguishing feature:** a small **silver ring on his left index finger**, ungendered style, plain band. Graduation gift from his sister, 2019. He has worn it every day for nearly seven years. It is the single object on his body that does not feel like administrative overhead. Mechanically useful: the ring can visually flicker/disappear/return as an anchor-state signal across the demo, and its loss can be an art beat without a line of dialogue.

**Clothing palette (pre-dungeon, baseline):**

- **Weekday:** soft cotton oxfords in slate, dusty blue, or faded charcoal, always slightly wrinkled. Plain chinos or slim-straight trousers in navy or stone. A single grey 优衣库 cashmere-blend cardigan he bought in 2023 and now wears November–April. Brown leather belt, scuffed. Black leather sneakers (低奢 domestic brand, not 奢侈品), worn.
- **Weekend:** same shirts untucked with soft joggers. A hoodie from his HUST grad program that he's kept out of inertia, not loyalty.
- **Inside the 副本:** the dream welds him into the 亭子间, which means his clothes acquire a 1990s–2010s shikumen register — the soft shirt reads as a tenant's undershirt, the cardigan as borrowed, the trousers as period-compatible enough. Art team can use this: his modern clothes and the dream's clothes are *almost* the same, just registered differently by the space. One small anachronism (the silver ring) is the only thing that doesn't translate.

**Palette logic overall:** low-saturation neutrals — slate, stone, dusty blue, warm grey, cream. No reds, no brights, no statement pieces. He reads as a watercolor background until someone looks at him directly.

**Expression default:** **mild attention.** Not sad, not tired, not blank. *Attending politely to a life adjacent to his own.* Artist reference: the specific face of someone listening to a colleague describe their weekend while calculating their own laundry schedule.

## 11. Archetype Anchor + Differentiation

**Closest archetype:** The **清冷现代知识分子受** from modern danmei — the clean-cut, cerebral, slightly-melancholic urban professional uke of mid-2010s to mid-2020s CN BL. Think the quiet, over-educated, under-lived office-intellectual lineage. In JP-BL adjacency, this maps to the softspoken-smart office uke from the *Ten Count* era (minus the overt neurosis coding).

**The ONE thing that differentiates him from the generic version:**

**He is not repressed. He is withdrawn.** The generic 清冷 archetype is emotionally constipated — feelings packed down by temperament, class, or trauma. MC's feelings are *intact*; he has simply stopped routing them to any exterior behavior. The dungeon does not need to *unlock* him (the 解冻 arc is the generic shape). It needs to *rejoin* him to actions he is already fluent in performing internally.

This matters mechanically: a generic 清冷 受's romance arc is about warming him up, teaching him to feel. MC's arc is about getting him to *act on what he already feels*. The LI is not melting him. The LI is translating him back into motion.

Writers working on him should feel this in dialogue: he *knows* what he feels and can often articulate it dryly. The drama is not discovery; the drama is application. This is the specific differentiation that will let readers who are tired of the 清冷 archetype still engage with him, and that will let EN readers (who are especially tired of "repressed intellectual thaws" as a pattern) find him contemporary.

## 12. Hard Guardrails (Confirmation)

- **No 金手指.** MC has no latent supernatural ability. He is a data analyst with a good observational eye, a decent ear for patterns, and no special relationship to the dream prior to entry. His advantage is attention, not power.
- **Not helpless.** He has a real adult life — job, rent, fluency, competence. He is tired and *under-capable for this specific situation*, which is a specific kind of vulnerability: a man who would be fine in most rooms, who is not fine in this one.
- **No 死亡万花筒 complementary-sense ability.** He does not have a supernatural sensory gift that pairs with LI's. Different IP; different genre lane. His differentiation from Lin Qiushi is explicit: where Lin Qiushi is a *veteran who regains memory*, MC is a *first-timer whose memory is the problem in the first place.*
- **Not cheerful-uke-dumb.** If his voice reads gentle or soft, it is exhaustion, not naïveté. He understands his situation. He is not enjoying it. When he is kind to strangers (and he is), it is from a remaining well of decency, not from a personality trait of "being sweet."
