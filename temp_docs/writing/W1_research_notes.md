# W1 — Research Notes for Shanghai Shikumen 无限流 BL VN

Research support package for the Narrative Designer working on `W1_world_bible.md`. Reference document, not prescription. Cites external sources for every load-bearing claim. CN terms kept in Chinese with EN gloss.

## 1. 无限流 (Unlimited/Infinite Flow) — Genre Conventions

### Origin
The genre was founded by 《无限恐怖》 (Terror Infinity) by **zhttty**, serialized on 起点 (Qidian) starting **2007**. Widely cited as the first work to fuse (a) instance-dungeon mechanics borrowed from MMO game design, (b) a central "main god space" (主神空间) hub world, and (c) a horror/survival framing where failure means death. Considered the first truly "web-native" (网生) Chinese online fiction genre.

### BL Benchmark
《死亡万花筒》 (Kaleidoscope of Death) by **西子绪**, serialized on JJWXC, **Jun 2018 – Jul 2018** (875,896 chars). The canonical BL 无限流 because:
- It imported 无限流's "doors/instances" structure into a modern-urban horror frame — each 门 (door) is a discrete survival scenario with its own rules.
- The CP (Lin Qiushi / Yuan Nanzhu) is built on **complementary supernatural senses** — one hears what others can't, one sees what others can't. This CP-as-toolkit pattern has since become a genre default for BL 无限流.
- It runs on meta-progression: the Black Obsidian organization, memory gaps, and a mystery about the nature of the doors. Readers expect layered answers, not single-instance payoffs.
- Adapted as audio drama (NetEase Cloud Music / Bianj Studio, 2018+, 47 eps across 3 seasons) and the live-action *Deadly Game* (2024).

### 2024–2026 JJWXC / Haitang 无限流 BL — representative active titles
| Title | Author | Position |
|---|---|---|
| 《全球高考》 | 木苏里 | Often cited as *the* "classic modern 无限流" alongside 死亡万花筒. Instances styled as high-risk exam subjects. Strong/strong CP, memory-gap mystery. |
| 《无限副本唯一指定清道夫》 | Philoso | Active 2024–2025. Strong/strong, thriller register, non-Chinese-named gong. Signals the "international instance crew" variant. |
| 《我要开始装逼了【无限流】》 | 天门即我 | Active to May 2025. Explicitly markets **规则怪谈** (rule-horror) alongside 爽文 (satisfaction-plot) elements. |
| 《穿进无限流的我失忆了?》 | 麦克疯 | Active to Feb 2025. The "穿进无限流" meta-variant — protagonist knows the genre. |
| 《无限流，但成为观众》 | 剪灯 | Late 2024. POV-inversion variant: protagonist is a viewer/audience, not a player. |

Haitang carries the higher-heat / more explicit variants but surfaces the same structural tropes; it is the overflow valve for material that won't pass JJWXC censorship.

### Stable genre rules (the "chassis")
1. **系统 (System)** — a non-negotiable arbiter that issues tasks, tracks points/lives/items, and enforces rules. Non-anthropomorphized; its voice is flat. Breaking rules = death or point loss.
2. **副本 (Instance)** — a discrete spatial/temporal unit with its own rules and win condition. Instances end; players return to a hub.
3. **Reward/punishment loop** — clearing an instance grants points/items/abilities; failing kills or debuffs. Sustains 200+ chapter length.
4. **新人 vs 老玩家** — rookie/veteran hierarchy is structural. Old players know the meta, own rare items, and can gatekeep or mentor. Biggest source of CP tension in BL 无限流.
5. **Meta-progression** — something larger than the instance is going on. The system's origin, the hub's nature, or a hidden adversary — eventually revealed.

### Sub-tropes by instance flavor
- **恐怖副本** (horror) — the 死亡万花筒 lane. Rule-based, psychological, often domestic/liminal settings.
- **末世副本** (apocalypse/post-apoc) — zombies, radiation, ruined cities. Declining in BL; readers call it fatigued.
- **科幻副本** (sci-fi) — space stations, simulated worlds, AI adversaries. Often paired with 强强 dynamics.
- **文学副本** (literary/classic-novel-based) — instances based on 红楼梦, 聊斋, 金瓶梅 etc. Prestige play; niche but durable.
- **规则怪谈副本** (rule-weird-tales) — the 2022–2025 hot subgenre. Survival hinges on parsing written rules placed in-world; rules may contradict or contain traps. **Directly relevant to a shikumen-with-rules premise.**

### What reads as 老套 (tired) in 2024–2026
- **System-issued points shops** with generic RPG items.
- **Zombie apocalypse instances** as default opener.
- **Protagonist with a golden finger** revealed chapter 1 (天生特殊体质, 穿越自带系统).
- **Generic "wicked big bad" final boss** with no thematic connection to the instances.
- **CP formed through one instance of rescue** — readers want relationship earned through sustained competence, not rescue romance.
- **Exposition-heavy system dialogue** — readers skip it; current works hide rules in environmental text.
- **No real consequences** — if deaths are always reversed, the stakes deflate.

## 2. Shanghai Shikumen (石库门) — Architecture + Social History

### Period & origin
Shikumen first appeared in Shanghai's British and French Concessions in the **1860s–1870s**, driven by refugees fleeing the **Taiping Rebellion (1850–64)** and the **Small Swords Society Uprising (1853–55)**. Foreign-concession developers adapted the Jiangnan courtyard house for dense row-house construction. By the 1930s, roughly two-thirds of Shanghai residents lived in shikumen; in the early 1950s, ~9,000 shikumen complexes comprised ~65% of residential stock.

### Architectural elements

- **石库门 (shíkùmén) — the stone gate**: glossy-black twin wooden doors set in a carved stone frame. Typical width ~1.4 m, height ~2.8 m. The frame gives the style its name — *shí* (stone) + *kù* (storehouse/frame) + *mén* (gate).
- **天井 (tiānjǐng) — front courtyard**: a small well-of-sky, smaller than a 四合院 courtyard. Admits light, air, and rain. Often a water vat, plants, laundry lines. Flanked by:
- **厢房 (xiāngfáng) — wings/side rooms**: left and right of the courtyard, typically bedrooms.
- **客堂 / 客堂间 (kètáng jiān) — parlor/central hall**: ~12 m², facing the 天井, most formal room. Ancestral tablet placed here traditionally. Used for receiving guests, family meals, weddings, funerals.
- **后天井 (hòu tiānjǐng) — back courtyard**: about half the size of the front one; historically houses the well.
- **灶披间 (zàopī jiān) — kitchen annex**: rear ground-floor, often a lean-to behind the back courtyard.
- **亭子间 (tíngzi jiān) — mezzanine room**: a small, low-ceilinged room **above the kitchen, between the first and second floors**, facing **north**. Universally described as the worst room: cold in winter, hot in summer, dim light. Historically occupied by servants, then by poor writers — hence the literary trope of the **"亭子间文人"** (mezzanine-room literati) of 1920s–40s Shanghai. **This room is a gift for a horror VN: architectural liminality baked in.**
- **晒台 (shàitái) — rooftop drying terrace**: flat or semi-flat, for laundry and summer sleeping. Post-war, illegal add-ons accumulated here.
- **后门 (hòumén) — back door**: service entrance onto the rear lane. In subdivided shikumen, a second family often controlled this door.
- **老虎窗 (lǎohǔchuāng) — "tiger windows"**: dormer windows in the sloped tile roof. Distinctive rooftop silhouette.

Exteriors read Western: red brick walls, arched stone gate frames with carved lintels, sloped tile roofs. Interiors are **Jiangnan**: wooden stair, courtyard-organized rooms, wooden shutters. Architectural emblem of treaty-port hybrid modernity.

### Post-1949 evolution — "一家变多家"
Private housing was nationalized in the 1950s; a shikumen designed for a single extended family was reassigned to multiple unrelated households. Subdividers ("二房东", second landlords) built lean-tos in courtyards, partitioned 客堂 with wooden boards, converted 亭子间 into whole-family homes. The comedic-but-accurate shorthand is **《七十二家房客》** ("The 72 Tenant Families", 1958 stage play, multiple film adaptations) — one shikumen, seventy-two families, every surface occupied.

Post-reform demolition: Xuhui District shikumen stock fell from 2.68 million m² (1949) to 0.25 million m² by late 1990s. Major razings include **Siwen Li** (Xinzha Rd, 706 residences, demolished 2012–14). A 2005 forced-demolition arson in Shanghai killed two elderly residents — violent displacement is a live historical note.

### 2020s cultural weight + surviving neighborhoods
The preservation model split into two templates:
- **新天地 (Xintiandi)** — commercial preservation. Luxury retail / dining on a fake-old facade. "Preserving the shell, killing the life."
- **田子坊 (Tianzifang)** — mixed-use in the former French Concession. Still has residents; arts-and-crafts enclave, tourist density, but not a clean scrub.

Heritage-protected shikumen precincts that still stand:
- **步高里 (Bugao Li / Cité Bourgogne)** — 87 two-story brick buildings, municipal heritage, still residential.
- **建业里 (Jianye Li)** — 260 residences; residents relocated 2003, redeveloped into luxury serviced apartments. *Warning case — preservation-by-gutting.*
- **上贤坊 (Shangxian Fang)** — heritage, converting to boutique hotels.
- **思明村, 梅兰坊, 荣康里** — protected.
- **愚园路 (Yuyuan Road area)** — the French Concession arterial, 108+ old Western-style houses. The "living" preservation district.

## 3. CN Supernatural Traditions Applicable to Domestic / Liminal Spaces

### 魂 vs 魄 (hun vs po)
Foundational distinction:
- **魂 (hún)** — yang, ethereal, "cloud-soul." Leaves the body at death, ascends. Three of them: 生魂 (vital), 觉魂 (awareness/emotion), 灵魂 (spiritual consciousness).
- **魄 (pò)** — yin, corporeal, "white-soul." Stays with the corpse. Seven of them. Regulates instinct, appetite, autonomic function.

A 魂 that cannot leave (died violent, died unburied, died with unfinished business) remains. A 魄 that was not properly sent off can turn 鬼. **Shikumen under 72-家房客 conditions, Cultural Revolution suicides, WWII bombings — all produce the conditions for lingering 魂/魄.**

### Taxonomy: 鬼 / 妖 / 精 / 怪
- **鬼 (guǐ)** — spirit of something *that was once alive* (human, animal). Always post-mortem. Benevolent (ancestor) or malevolent (wronged dead). Shape: usually recognizable as their former self.
- **妖 (yāo)** — a natural being (animal, plant) that has cultivated enough 灵气 to gain consciousness, shape-shifting, magic. Centuries of development, moral landscape, often cultivates toward immortality. Fox / snake spirit lineage. **妖 can be a sympathetic LI archetype** — tradition supports it.
- **精 (jīng)** — like 妖 but earlier-stage. An object or animal with nascent consciousness. Less powerful, less morally developed.
- **怪 (guài)** — anomaly. Inanimate becomes conscious, or something breaks the rules of the natural world. Moral category is neutral — 怪 is just *wrong*. Terrifying precisely because it isn't person-shaped.

### 宅神 / 家神 — domestic tutelary spirits
Every traditional Chinese home has a pantheon of resident deities:
- **灶神 / 灶王爷** — the Stove God. Lives in the 灶披间. Reports annually to the Jade Emperor on household conduct.
- **门神 (Mén Shén)** — door gods. Pasted on the outside of the door. Ward off malevolent 鬼. A shikumen's 石库门 is literally the installation point.
- **土地公 (Tǔdì Gōng)** — Earth God / locality god. Tutelary of a specific neighborhood / block. Every 里弄 historically had a small 土地庙 shrine. When the neighborhood is demolished, the 土地公 is architecturally displaced.
- **财神** — wealth god. Often enshrined in the 客堂.
- **祖先牌位 (ancestor tablets)** — not deities per se. Offerings at festivals.

### 阴气 (yīn qì) — how domestic spaces accumulate it
阴气 is the yin energy of coldness, darkness, stillness, death. Spaces accumulate it through:
- **Lack of sunlight** — north-facing rooms (see 亭子间), basements, enclosed courtyards.
- **Death events** — the more violent and the less properly buried, the more.
- **Abandonment** — a house without 阳气 tilts toward yin.
- **Water** — wells, wet walls. Water is yin.
- **Thresholds and liminal surfaces** — 门槛 (thresholds), stairs, backward-facing windows, north-facing openings — all traditionally sites where yin can enter. The 门槛 must be stepped *over*, never *on*, or you invite spirits in.

### Why shikumen hauntings differ from 四合院 / 土楼
- **四合院 (Beijing courtyard)** — a wealthy single-family configuration. Hauntings here are **ancestral** — the wronged concubine, the filial dispute, the family curse.
- **土楼 (Fujian Hakka roundhouse)** — fortress-like, communal, clan-based. Hauntings are **collective** — clan feuds, inheritance wars, wartime massacres.
- **石库门** — a *hybrid, dense, post-1949 subdivided* form. Hauntings here are **urban-modern**: suicides in the Cultural Revolution, WWII family tragedies, servants who died in the 亭子间, forced relocations, the dead of 72-家房客 overcrowding. The haunting is **not ancestral but demographic** — waves of forgotten residents layered on top of each other. The 亭子间 specifically is a cold, cramped, north-facing room occupied by servants and impoverished writers — a concentrated 阴气 site by design.

### What makes an entity "feel CN" vs Western demon / JP yōkai / KR goblin
- vs **Western demon** — Western demons are metaphysical (fallen angels, elemental evil). CN 鬼/妖 are almost always **formerly something specific** — a named person, a particular fox on a particular mountain. Biographical, not cosmic.
- vs **JP yōkai** — yōkai are whimsical, taxonomized, fixed-appearance. CN 妖/精 are **morally ambiguous and individually drawn**.
- vs **KR dokkaebi** — dokkaebi are trickster-prankster figures. CN tradition weights **cultivation (修炼)** — spirits progress over time.

A CN supernatural entity for this project should have: (a) a specific biography (who they were before), (b) a cultivation arc (how they got their current power), (c) moral complexity, (d) a visible pull toward **执念** (fixation/obsession) — the attachment that keeps them on the boundary.

## 4. Real Shanghai Urban Legends + Haunted Buildings

- **武康大楼 / The Normandie** (1842–1858 Huaihai Rd). Hudec-designed 1924 art deco residential. Known in the Cultural Revolution as **"跳水板"** (the diving board) for the number of persecuted intellectuals who jumped. Actress **上官云珠** died here 1968. Archetype: **CR-era suicide haunting**.
- **百乐门 / The Paramount** (218 Yuyuan Rd). 1930s nightclub. Dancer **陈曼丽** shot in **1941** for refusing to dance with a Japanese officer. Archetype: **wartime-violence haunting**.
- **邱家大宅 / Qiu Mansion** (Weihai Rd). Twin mansions built by merchant brothers, housed exotic animals. Workers demolishing it in 2010 reported mysterious animal-bite wounds. Archetype: **wealth-and-excess haunting**.
- **静安公园** — originally **Bubbling Well Road Cemetery**, 1890s–1955. Archetype: **buried-over burial ground**.
- **龙柱 (Dragon Pillar, Yan'an Elevated Hwy interchange)** — urban legend that construction in the 1990s couldn't drive piles through a specific point. Archetype: **geomantic site of modernization**.
- **林家宅37号** — widely circulated "1956 Shanghai haunted house" story. Multiple CN sources confirm this is **fictional**, originating from a web novel. Useful as aesthetic reference.

Folklore associations with architectural features:
- **北房 (north-facing rooms)** — chronically dim, cold. The 亭子间 is canonically north-facing. Yin accumulates.
- **门槛 (thresholds)** — must be stepped over, never on.
- **后门 (back doors)** — spirits prefer back entrances. Folk practice leaves offerings at back doors.
- **井 (wells)** — yin-charged. Wells figure heavily in CN horror.
- **镜子 (mirrors)** — traditionally cover mirrors during a death. Mirrors can trap 魂.

Historical tragedy layers applicable to any shikumen neighborhood:
- **1937–1941** — Japanese invasion/occupation. Family separations, disappearances, domestic violence.
- **1950s** — property nationalization. Original owner families dispossessed.
- **1966–1976 Cultural Revolution** — struggle sessions in the 客堂, suicides (often by jumping from 晒台 terraces), family denunciations.
- **1990s–2010s** — demolition waves. Forced relocations, arson (2005), community dissolution.

## 5. Monster / Non-Human LI Conventions in CN Danmei

### The central reference set
- **花城 (Hua Cheng) from 《天官赐福》** — 800-year-old 鬼王, Crimson Rain Sought Flower. Reigning archetype for "devoted non-human gong." Key traits: exceptional competence, long wait, remembers what the human protagonist forgot, **chooses devotion** (wasn't born into it).
- **魏无羡 from 《魔道祖师》** — not strictly non-human but the 夷陵老祖 / resurrected-in-another-body / corpse-cultivation association trades on it. Establishes the "morally complex, demonized by society, devoted in private" pattern.
- **《二哈和他的白猫师尊》 (2HA) / 肉包不吃肉** — xianxia register. Shares the "power differential + centuries of waiting" structure.
- **沈巍 from 《镇魂》 / Priest** — a 10,000-year-old 鬼王 (Envoy of the Underworld) posing as a mild-mannered professor. **Modern-urban supernatural — closest register to this VN.**

### Reader expectations for a 非人攻 in modern danmei 2024–2026
- **Power competence is non-negotiable.** The non-human gong should be the most capable entity in the room by a margin.
- **Devotion must be earned, not congenital.** "He was destined to love me" is tired. "He chose me, over centuries, through things he refuses to explain" is the current shape.
- **Power differential is real but not weaponized against the shou.** Against enemies — yes. Against the shou — never.
- **Specific biography.** What century were they active? What do they know that's now obsolete? What did they lose?
- **Legible physical markers.** Red eyes, specific scars, a prop, distinct clothing palette. Readers make fanart; give them something to draw.
- **Vulnerability has to exist.** An invincible gong is boring. Usually: a specific object, promise, or memory is the weakness.

### Sympathetic monster vs cringe monster
- **Sympathetic**: specific backstory of wrong; cultivation that cost something; moral code (often stricter than humans'); capacity for tenderness toward one specific person.
- **Cringe**: vague "dark past"; omnipotence without cost; possessive behavior coded as romantic; forced mating/mpreg imported from Western omegaverse without CN grounding; "cool" aesthetics without biographical grounding.

**Most load-bearing CN-specific convention**: the non-human LI's attachment is read as **执念 (zhíniàn)** — a Buddhist-influenced term that is morally complicated. 执念 is what keeps a spirit bound; it's also what makes them capable of deep love.

## 6. Time-of-Day Symbolism (for the One-Night Clock)

### The 十二时辰 system

| 时辰 | Hours | Literary name | Register |
|---|---|---|---|
| 子 zǐ | 23:00–01:00 | 夜半 / 子夜 (midnight) | Deepest yin. Spirits strongest. |
| 丑 chǒu | 01:00–03:00 | **鸡鸣** (rooster-crow) | First rooster. Boundary. |
| 寅 yín | 03:00–05:00 | 平旦 / 黎明 (dawn-break) | Sky lightens. Yin receding. |
| 卯 mǎo | 05:00–07:00 | **日出** (sunrise) | Sun fully up. Yang ascendant. |
| 辰 chén | 07:00–09:00 | 食时 (meal time) | Full daylight. |

### 鸡鸣 as supernatural-boundary signal
The rooster's crow announces that yang is returning. First crow ~子时, second ~丑时, third ~寅时 when 天将明. A rooster crowing at the wrong time — middle of the dead of night — is called **荒鸡 (huāng jī, "wild rooster")** and is inauspicious; it means spirits have disturbed natural order. **Rooster crow = the veil closing.**

### Literary register differences for "dawn"
- **日出 (rìchū)** — "sun emerges." Concrete, observational.
- **天明 (tiānmíng)** — "heaven-bright." Formal, classical. Poetry.
- **破晓 (pòxiǎo)** — "break-dawn." Action-coded, dramatic. Modern fiction for the moment light cracks.
- **黎明 (límíng)** — "pre-dawn light." The hour before sunrise.
- **拂晓 (fúxiǎo)** — "brushing dawn." Military/classical, the hour armies attack.

For this VN: **破晓** is the right register for the climactic moment. **拂晓** for a melancholic/classical note. **天明** to emphasize cosmic resolution.

### Grounding for a "one-night clock ending at dawn"
- 子时 (23:00) as the *entry* point — liminal door opens.
- 丑时 (01:00) — first rooster, first boundary test; spirits react here.
- 寅时 (03:00) — the "darkest before dawn" beat; most supernatural ambushes in folk tales happen here.
- 卯时 (05:00–07:00) — sunrise, safety. Any spirit still bound at 卯时 is in trouble.
- **荒鸡** at any *wrong* moment (mid-子时) = instance's rules have been violated.

Free, culturally-grounded tension clock. CN readers will intuit stakes without exposition.

## 7. References Regina Should Be Aware Of

### Inspiration / reference (lean into)
- **《死亡万花筒》 — 西子绪** — the BL 无限流 canon. The CP sensory complementarity, doors-as-instances structure, and "organization with hidden agenda" frame all lifted from here. Reading it in full is research-worthy.
- **《天官赐福》 — 墨香铜臭** — ur-reference for "devoted non-human gong" dynamics. Hua Cheng's narrative architecture (long wait, hidden biography, specific physical markers) is the template readers will measure any 鬼王-type LI against.

### Adjacent territory / red flag (risk of overlap)
- **《镇魂》 — Priest** — modern-urban Chinese supernatural with a 鬼王 LI. Closest genre-adjacent CP register to a shikumen-BL-horror VN. *Red flag*: if the gong is "mild modern professional + secretly 10,000-year-old 鬼王," readers will compare directly to Shen Wei. Differentiate by making the non-human register more embodied (not passing for human all the time) and by grounding the setting in Shanghai-specific architecture rather than a fictional city.
- **《规则怪谈：商场》 + the 规则怪谈 Steam-game wave (2023–2025)** — rule-horror in a confined urban space. Very close to the likely mechanic of a "shikumen with rules" VN. *Red flag*: the "read the rules on the wall, don't trust the rules" loop is now highly genre-recognizable. Rules should be shikumen-specific (thresholds, 亭子间, 鸡鸣 timing) rather than generic mall-signage.

### Architectural / atmospheric reference
- **Wong Kar-wai — *In the Mood for Love* (花样年华, 2000) + *2046***. Not shikumen (1960s HK tenements — parallel lilong-lineage form), but the atmospheric register — dense overcrowded lane-house life, laundry lines, narrow corridors, cigarette smoke, women in qipao on narrow stairs — is the visual-reference gold standard. Art team should mine frames.
- **Jia Zhangke — *24 City* (二十四城记, 2008)**. For the tone of a neighborhood being demolished — resident interviews layered on demolition footage. Grounds the emotional weight of "the neighborhood itself is already half-gone."

## 8. One-Line Implications for the World Bible

1. **The 无限流 chassis is now a genre with tired tropes, not a fresh form** — design around the known fatigue (no generic zombies, no golden-finger openers, no RPG point-shops). Hide the rules in environmental text; don't have the system narrate.
2. **The 亭子间 is the world bible's free gift** — a cramped, cold, north-facing, historically liminal room literally designed into shikumen architecture. Use it as a narrative node.
3. **The 石库门 gate is both a physical gate and a cosmological threshold** — historically the home's first line of defense (门神 pasted on it), and as the VN's entry point it is already culturally legible as "crossing over."
4. **Layer historical tragedies into the neighborhood — don't invent new ones.** CR suicides, WWII separations, 72-家房客 overcrowding, and 2000s forced demolitions all produce architecturally-specific hauntings.
5. **Use 十二时辰 for the one-night clock with 鸡鸣 as boundary signal.** 子时 entry → 丑时 first rooster → 寅时 ambush hour → 卯时 dawn/escape. Zero exposition needed for CN readers. **荒鸡** = rule violation marker.
6. **The non-human LI needs biography, cultivation arc, 执念, and legible physical markers** — not "cool dark aura." Readers will compare to Hua Cheng and Shen Wei; differentiate by grounding the LI's backstory in *this shikumen neighborhood*.
7. **Distinguish 鬼 / 妖 / 精 / 怪 in the monster cast** — don't make everything "a ghost." A shikumen can plausibly host a human 鬼 (former resident), a 妖 (an alley cat that cultivated for a century), a 精 (an old mirror in the 客堂 that woke up), and a 怪 (the stairwell that shouldn't exist).
8. **Every architectural feature has folkloric weight** — 北房 yin, 门槛 threshold, 后门 spirit entrance, 井 yin-water, 镜子 soul-trap, 灶 stove-god. When writing a rule, check if it can be phrased in this vocabulary — it almost always can, and will read as culturally native rather than imported horror.
