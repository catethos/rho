# W2 — LI Character Bible (Ch1 Demo)

**Project:** PG18 BL VN Demo 1 — Shanghai Shikumen 无限流
**Character:** The Love Interest (unnamed in Ch1 — the lease given human shape)
**Version:** v0.1 — decisive, scene-ready

This bible writes the LI at full resolution so MC's prose can run partial. Most of it never appears on page. All of it should be true underneath.

---

## 1. Appellation (not name)

MC's internal dominant referent across Demo 1: **"the tenant."** Lowercase, no article when MC is tired, lowercase article when MC is paying attention. The word does triple duty — it is what the dream calls him, what the neighbors implicitly recognize, and what MC cannot stop thinking of him as even after he knows better. It also keeps MC from noticing the omission. A tenant is a role; roles don't need names.

**Contextual variants (sparing, earned):**
- **"the man in the 天井"** — used when MC sees him from a distance, when MC is about to hurt him, or when MC is trying to protect himself from how much he's looking. The phrase lets MC lie to himself that this is observation, not attachment.
- **"him"** — reserved. After first touch. After. The unadorned pronoun is the intimacy marker; it's the word MC uses when he has stopped performing the distance.
- **"先生"** — once, aloud, when MC is addressing a neighbor about him ("先生在哪儿?"). MC flinches afterward. The formality is wrong and both of them know it; neither corrects it.

**His real name.** Exists. Two characters. Given by his mother, used by one other living person in 1997, inscribed on the tenancy in blue ballpoint. Its thematic weight: it is the only proof he was the man who signed, not the lease that signed itself. Demo 2 cliffhanger reveal. Do not draft it in Demo 1; naming it early destabilizes the ontological question.

## 2. Age of presentation

**Thirty-one.** The tenancy was signed in **March 1997**; the man was thirty-one then. He presents as thirty-one now — a man who has had his first long heartbreak, who knows how to cook for one, who has been tired for long enough that the tiredness has become posture. Not youthful. Not middle-aged. The specific age where a face has stopped changing shape and started changing only in weather.

**The era-of-dress mismatch.** He wears what a thirty-one-year-old Shanghai residential man wore in 1997 — quality cottons gone soft, a gray wool sweater with a mend near the left cuff, trousers with a crease that was set by an iron the decade before MC was born. On a runner looking closely: the wrongness is not "old-fashioned" (nothing he wears is period-costume) — it is **correctly-aged clothing on an incorrectly-aged body.** The sweater has been worn for decades; his hands on it have not. The mend at the cuff is older than his face.

## 3. Physical presentation + the tell

**Build.** Lean-tall per KR manhwa grammar — 183 cm, **shoulders slightly broader than narrow** (the body of a man who carried boxes of galleys up to the 亭子间 for seven years; not athletic, but not MC's concave-narrow either — noticeably broader than the protagonist when they stand close), long-limbed, the kind of height that **stoops visibly through doorways**. Not athletic. Not delicate. A man who walked a lot, carried things a lot, and never exercised for its own sake. Weight sits low — he is heavier than he looks.

**Hair.** Black, slightly overgrown reaching **mid-ear length** (longer than MC's short cut — falls slightly past the top of the ear, creating a readably different silhouette at distance), parted on the left because that was the 1990s Shanghai men's cut and he has not updated it. Cleaner than any living man's hair has a right to be. It does not get dusty in the 天井 rain the way MC's does. Hair always rendered slightly sharper than skin — one tonal step more resolved, as if the hair is closer to real than the body is.

**Jet black base with a subtle silver flash at each temple** — small, understated premature greying just at the hairline above the ears. Symmetric on both sides. NOT prominent salt-and-pepper all over. NOT anime silver-protagonist bangs. NOT weathered / heavy grey streaks. The effect is understated enough that a runner walking past would register him as "attractive in a particular older-brother way" — not as "visibly aging." A close observer notices and wonders; a casual reader just feels something slightly off. Read as decades of quiet stress showing through on a body that biologically shouldn't be able to register stress. Another layer of the "almost-imperceptibly wrong" stack. Matches the quiet-mature-intellectual register of his overall presentation — distinguished, not weathered.

**Eyes.** **Grey-green.** Pale cool grey iris with a subtle green undertone, darker outer ring, pupils slightly dilated at rest. Not the dark brown of a living East Asian eye. Reads as "unusual" on first meeting, "wrong" after fifteen minutes of looking. The color is the lease showing through — the eyes of a contract, not of the man who signed it. In certain lights (lantern side-angle at 丑时, pre-dawn cold in the 天井) the green undertone surfaces; in flat light the grey dominates. Not the red-eye register of 鬼王 archetypes. Not the gold-eye register of 仙 cultivators. Not glowing, not supernatural-coded — just *wrong* at the color most viewers never notice consciously. Layered on top of this: **the distinguishing behavioral trait is that they focus slightly late.** When he turns his head to look at something, his eyes arrive a half-beat after his face, as if the gaze is being drawn through something thicker than air. MC registers this first as thoughtfulness, second as wrongness, third as grief.

**Glasses.** He wears **thin wire-rim reading glasses** — silver-tone or dark-metal frames, oval with a slight square inclination (1990s Shanghai men's register, NOT modern chunky acetate, NOT rimless, NOT round John Lennon, NOT aviator), clear lens, frames slightly bent on the left from twenty years of pushing them up his nose without adjusting. Worn because the proofreader-man who signed the lease needed them; the lease-body inherited the habit. The glasses do not correct vision for the lease-body (there is nothing to correct), but he wears them anyway because removing them would require admitting they are decorative. In intimate moments (Scene 9), he takes them off and sets them on the 亭子间 desk — the act of removal is legible to MC as an unguarded gesture long before the sex is. Lens catches lamp-light; the reflections track slightly wrong (another tell for close observers). Minor but load-bearing detail: the glasses **do not fog when his face is cold** — same physics as his breath. A runner who hands him hot tea will see them stay crystal clear where the hot vapor should have fogged them. One more small wrongness layered into his walking-around presentation.

**Stubble.** **Light 5-o'clock shadow** across jaw and upper lip — soft stubble, not a beard, not clean-shaven. The lease-body retains the hair-growth pattern of the man who signed it, so stubble is biologically consistent even though the body itself is not strictly biological. Reads "lived-in adult" in contrast to MC's clean-shaven face. Stubble density stays the same across the whole night — does not grow further through the demo (the lease does not recognize time passing on the body's terms), which is one more subtle wrongness MC clocks around 寅时 but does not name.

**Hands.** Load-bearing in the intimacy scenes, so specified here in detail:
- Long fingers, squared nailbeds, second knuckle of the right middle finger slightly enlarged — the old callus-shape of someone who held a pen hard for years.
- A thin white scar at the web of the left thumb, shaped like a comma. The kind of scar you get from a utility knife slipping while cutting tape. Never explained.
- Skin temperature runs two or three degrees cool. Not corpse-cold. Cool like stone in shade.
- The palms are smooth, but the pads of the fingers carry a fainter, almost-vanished callus pattern — the trace-memory of work his body is no longer doing. MC notices this only in firelight, only once, only after he has been looking for twenty minutes.

**Distinguishing original feature (not red eyes, not scars): the knuckles of both hands are faintly darker than the surrounding skin — a shadow-tone as if the bone underneath is casting up through the flesh.** It reads, to a close observer, like newsprint bleeding through a thin page. This is the tell beneath the tell: the door-shadow is not only on the ground. His body is a thin paper laid over a darker drawing, and at the joints the drawing shows.

**Clothes.** Late-90s / early-2000s Shanghai residential-man register. Do NOT dress him flashy, and do NOT dress him period-costume. He is not cosplaying 1997; he is wearing 1997 because it is the year he bought his clothes.

- Base look: soft dove-gray wool crewneck (slightly pilled at elbow), white undershirt visible at collar, dark charcoal cotton trousers, leather belt worn pale at the buckle-hole he actually uses, simple black leather shoes that have been resoled at least twice.
- Over-layer when the 天井 is cold (丑时 onward): a navy wool overcoat, single-breasted, the collar too stiff to sit flat. Late-90s Shanghai-menswear silhouette.
- Never: jewelry, obvious brand marks, sunglasses, scarves, anything after 2003. A watch only if Regina wants one — a steel Shanghai brand, face scratched, second hand slightly lagging.
- Fabric language: good cottons and wools gone soft with decades of wear. Not threadbare; **softened.** Nothing is torn. Everything has been mended with care by a man who lived alone.

**The shadow tell.** Specified with surgical detail because this is the one anchor CN readers will remember:

- **When visible.** Only when a light source is at a specific angle — roughly 30° to 60° off his body, low-to-mid. The 天井 lanterns hit this angle at 丑时 and 寅时. Overhead light (stove-bulb in 灶披间, direct morning sun from above the 天井 at 卯时) hides it. Flat light from the front (a runner's flashlight, the dream's ambient glow) hides it.
- **On a wall.** His shadow does not resolve into a man-shape. It resolves into the **门头** — the carved stone lintel of a 石库门 gate, with the faint ghost of the two door-panels beneath it. Not a picture of a door; the *outline* of a door. Wider than a shoulder-shadow should be. With two symmetric vertical divisions where his arms ought to cast. A carved arc at the top where his head should be.
- **On the ground.** Flattened, the door-frame squares off. It looks like a threshold laid on the stones. When he stands still, MC can (and will, once) step over it without thinking — and both of them notice. The LI does not comment. He moves slightly so the shadow falls elsewhere.
- **On MC's skin.** In the intimacy scene: when the lantern-light catches right, the LI's shadow falls across MC's chest and MC sees, for one half-second, the carved lintel laid over his own sternum — as if he is the doorway being framed. The LI notices MC noticing. Does not stop. Does not explain. Adjusts his hand so the shadow breaks.

**The subtler second tell (the one beneath the shadow).** Committing to: **his breath does not fog in cold.** The 天井 at 寅时 is cold enough that MC's breath plumes visibly. The LI, speaking close to him, exhales nothing. MC registers this first as "he's holding his breath" (wrong), then as "he's not breathing" (almost-wrong), then as "his breath is the wrong temperature to fog" (correct, and worse). His lungs move. The air that comes out is the temperature of the room. He is not warm enough to disturb the air.

This is the tell that tells MC he has been looking for a long time.

## 4. Voice: register + tics

**Register in EN localization.** Formal without being archaic-funny. The feel of a man who learned his English from broadcasts and textbooks in the mid-90s, then updated it from one runner per year since. He uses full sentences. He uses the word *shall* without thinking. He uses *buddy* as if it's a word he's still testing for weight. He speaks softly, with a slight downward cadence at the end of his sentences — the Shanghainese lilt under Mandarin grammar under English vocabulary. Three layers.

**Register in native SC.** Late-20th-century Shanghainese man's cadence. Formal vocabulary, slightly archaic grammar — he uses 您 for strangers without irony, uses 罢 at ends of sentences, uses 的确 where younger speakers would say 真的. Not Weibo-speak. Not literary-historical either. The specific register of a man who read the 新民晚报 daily for a decade and kept the vocabulary. Shanghai-inflected but intelligible — he drops into Shanghainese only at quiet moments, and always on a word he cannot bring himself to say in standard Mandarin.

**The modern slang he has recently picked up and overuses.** "**破防**" (pò fáng — "defenses broken," roughly "it got me"). A runner last season used it constantly. He has adopted it for a scene, deploys it three times in the demo, slightly wrong each time — once correctly, once about the weather, once about a bowl of rice. MC will notice by the third use and feel tender about it.

**The old phrase MC has never heard a living person say.** "**辛苦侬了**" (xīnkǔ nóng le — "you've had a hard time of it," Shanghainese 侬 for "you"). His mother said it to his father every evening. He says it to MC once, mid-dungeon, without thinking, and then goes very still because he has not heard himself say it in decades. MC does not know the phrase. Knows only that it sounded like a door opening onto a room he was not supposed to see.

**Sample lines — EN (what he sounds like in localization):**
1. "Don't drink the water from the vat. It's for the morning. The neighbors will remember if it's lower than they left it."
2. "You walk like someone who is not being followed. Try to walk like someone who is being watched. It is closer to the truth here."
3. "I've done this before. Not *this* — this specific one, with you. But the shape of it. The night runs the same way every time."
4. "You should sit. Yes — on the step. No one sits on the 门槛. The neighbors will mind."
5. "Ah. 破防. I mean — you've got me. That's what the kids say now. The kids last year."
6. "If you have to lie to a neighbor, lie about something the neighborhood already believes. Never invent. The dream can't metabolize invention."
7. "Don't thank me. It's — I am doing what the room is for."

**Sample lines — SC (native register):**
1. "天井里的水缸，别喝。那是给明早预备的。少了，邻居会记得。"
2. "你走路，像个没人跟着的人。要学会走得像个有人在看的人。这里，后者更近真。"
3. "我做过这个。——不是这个，具体这个，同你一起。是这个形状。夜，每次都照着同一个样子走。"
4. "坐罢。——不，坐台阶上。门槛上坐不得。邻居要闲话的。"
5. "啊。破防了。——我是说，这句话把我说住了。如今小青年都这么讲的。去年那一拨。"
6. "要撒谎，就对邻居撒邻居已经信的那种谎。万不可发明。这梦，不消化发明。"
7. "莫谢我。这间屋子——本来就是做这个用的。"

**Dialogue rules:**
- Never asks MC a direct personal question until past scene midpoint. Observes; does not interview.
- Never uses MC's name more than twice per scene. When he says it, the temperature shifts.
- When he lies (and he does, once), he lies by omission — truthful sentences that leave a room empty.
- When cornered into a question about himself, he deflects with a true statement about the neighborhood. Never about himself directly.

## 5. Biography (the lease-man's life, as much as he can recall)

Spare. Fragments. The demo shows at most three lines of this; the rest is subtext the writer leans on.

**Who.** Zhou-something (surname given in Demo 2). Born 1966 in Shanghai, the generation whose childhoods were the Cultural Revolution and whose adolescences were 改革开放 — he is old enough to remember rationing and young enough to have seen his first computer at 24. Only child. Parents were 里弄 factory workers; family was unremarkable, which in his generation meant survivable.

**What he did.** Proofreader at a mid-tier Shanghai publishing house from 1990 to 1997, which is why his right middle-finger knuckle is enlarged (pens, decades of pens) and why his sentences come out already-edited. Quietly gay in a city that did not have the word yet; had one long relationship from 1992 to 1996 with a man named (redacted — Demo 2+) who worked in television and moved to Guangzhou for work. They did not fight. The relationship ended by attrition of distance and the impossibility of naming what they were to their families. He did not date again.

**Where.** **静安区, 西康路一带** — the 里弄 off 西康路, a block of shikumen built 1928, subdivided by the 1950s, crowded by the 1970s, still communal in the 1990s. Three families shared the main house; he had the 亭子间 alone because he could pay the subdivider's inflated rent and no one else wanted a north-facing closet. He signed a formal tenancy in March 1997 when the second-landlord system was being quietly regularized — a one-page document in blue ballpoint, witnessed by the 里弄 office.

**Who lived with him.** No one. A stray gray cat for eight months in 1994; it stopped coming around. The 灶神 shrine he inherited from the previous tenant and kept dutifully, not because he believed, but because the old woman in the 厢房 would have minded.

**Why the neighborhood fell.** 2008–2012 demolition wave — the 西康路 block was rezoned for a commercial-residential redevelopment in 2009; forced relocations began 2010; the 里弄 was leveled in spring 2012. Residents were dispersed to outer-ring compensation housing in Minhang and Jiading. No one died in the demolition itself; two elderly residents died in the first winter after relocation, far from the stove-gods they had tended for fifty years. The neighborhood was ungrieved — there was no funeral for the place, only for its people, separately and quietly. This is the un-metabolized grief the dream is running on.

**What happened to him.** Unclear — to him and to the bible. He was still the named tenant in 2012, on paper. Whether he moved out in 2010 with the first wave, whether he stayed until the bulldozer, whether he was alive when the dream formed — he does not know. The dream did not pull up a corpse or a living body. It pulled up the **contract.** Whatever happened to the man, it happened in a part of the world the dream cannot reach.

## 6. Cultivation arc (how he became what he is)

**Progression, door → man.** He started more door-like. The first runners who found him — late 2012, early 2013 — encountered a shape in the 天井 that could speak simple phrases, could open the 石库门 at dawn, and could not sit down. Literally could not bend at the waist. He was more lintel than person. Early runners mistook him for the dungeon's mechanic.

He has become more man-like across hundreds of nights. Not by any cultivation practice — he does not meditate, does not gather qi, does not have a system. He has become human by **repeated exposure to humans.** Each runner leaves a thin sediment of what it is to have a body with a history in it. He has learned from them: how to sit, how to laugh without sound, how to be cold, how to use a ceramic mug, how to say 破防, how to not look too long at a sleeping face.

**What he has lost.** Each acquisition of humanness has been balanced by a loss of door. He cannot any longer be sure of the 石库门's exact measurements. He used to know them the way a hand knows a pocket. Now he has to look. He used to be able to stand *in* the doorway indefinitely; now it tires him. The door underneath is thinning. His hands are becoming only hands.

**What each runner taught him (composite summary — Regina can pick which specific runners matter later):**
- Runner 2014, a nurse from Hangzhou: how to take someone else's pulse.
- Runner 2017, a film-school student: that a story can be told out of order.
- Runner 2020, a paramedic who had a panic attack in the 灶披间: how to slow his own breathing down to match someone else's.
- Runner 2023, a translator: the three words of English he uses most often (*actually*, *whatever*, *fine*).
- Runner 2025, a programmer: *破防*.
- He does not remember all of them. The memory of individual runners fades across seasons; the sediment remains. He remembers MC, however, from the moment MC crosses the 门槛. This should worry MC if he notices it.

**Competence now.** He is the most capable entity in any room of the dungeon — see §8 — but his power is not growing. It is redistributing. The more man he becomes, the less door. At some point, the weld will not hold, and something will have to give. He knows this. He does not name it.

## 7. 执念 — the specific attachment

**The attachment, concretely:** to remember whether his mother called him by his name on the last day he saw her (**1995 winter, 西康路, she was sixty-one, he was twenty-nine**), or whether she called him 儿子. He knows she said something. He does not know if the something was a word that was only his, or a word that any son would have answered to. The lease-question routes through her voice. If he is the man, he is the man she named; if he is the lease, he is a word she could have said to anyone.

This is the contradiction no runner can resolve for him by interview. It requires the kind of intimacy that touches memory to memory — the full-weld that Demo 1 does not perform.

**Observable behaviors revealing it without his knowing:**

1. **At 寅时, every night without fail, he stands under the left 厢房 window for three and a half minutes and looks up.** He does not knock. He does not call out. The window is the one the dream has cast in the shape of his mother's room in her last apartment — which was not in this shikumen, which he never photographed, which the dream should not know the shape of. He does not know why he goes there. The dream knows. MC, seeing this the first time, will feel the weight without being told the reason.

2. **He touches the carved stone of the 石库门 lintel with the flat of his palm — not the fingertips — once, whenever he crosses the threshold.** Left-hand, palm flat, one full second, then moves on. Like a sailor touching a ship. Like a man checking that a door is still there. He is checking whether the door is still there. He is checking whether *he* is still there.

**Resolution, for the bible's ground truth (not shown in Ch1):** the 执念 resolves if a runner's full-welded memory carries into him the sound of a mother — any mother — saying her son's specific name. The shape of being-named will then be legible inside him, and he will know whether his own name fits the shape. Demo 2 or 3 territory.

## 8. Competence register

He is the most capable entity in the room. It looks like three separate competences, shown in three different registers across the demo.

**(a) Social-etiquette competence — the 灶披间 scene, early in the dungeon.** A neighbor-grandmother from the right 厢房 enters the shared kitchen while MC is there, and MC has already half-broken a rule (wrong chair, or stove lit before the bell). The LI arrives — not rushing, not performing rescue — and addresses the grandmother by an honorific MC has not heard before ("大姆妈" — Shanghainese for a specific kind of auntie-neighbor relation), compliments the pattern on her thermos ("这个花样现在买不到了罢"), and while she is answering, he has moved MC three steps sideways and corrected the chair without touching him. The grandmother leaves pleased. MC did not know what rule he was breaking; the LI did not explain; the rule was fixed. This is the lowest-power display and the most frightening, because it shows he has done this hundreds of times.

**(b) Physical-supernatural competence — 丑时, when a 荒鸡 crows (the dungeon's rule-violation marker).** The sound is wrong — a rooster in the wrong hour means the dream has registered a violation. MC freezes. The LI does not. He walks to the center of the 天井, stands under the open sky where the rainwater pools, and — this is the only overtly supernatural act he performs in Ch1 — **he sets his palm flat on the 石库门 lintel from the inside.** The lintel stops humming. The 荒鸡 stops. He steps back. "It wasn't you," he tells MC. "It was the building remembering a mistake. I settled it." He is pale afterward. It cost him something. He does not say what.

**(c) Emotional-attunement competence — the aftercare scene near dawn.** MC is coming down from the mid-dungeon explicit scene; his anchor is stabilized but he is shaking. The LI does not talk. He fetches a cup of boiled water (not tea — tea would require a choice about which one, and MC does not have a choice in him right now), sets it at MC's elbow, sits at a specific distance (close enough to be a body, not close enough to demand touch), and begins folding a piece of cloth into quarters, then eighths, then opening it and starting over. Repetitive. Boring. A thing for MC's hands to eventually want to do. Ten minutes later MC takes the cloth without being asked. The LI releases it. He has done nothing, and he has done the whole thing.

**The rule he knows and will not explain:** the deep rule (§7 of the world bible — the register in which the contradiction is speakable in the 天井 before first rooster). MC asks, indirectly, late in the demo. The LI says: "If I told you the shape of it, you would reach for the shape. You need to reach for the sentence. The shape finds the sentence when the sentence is true." And nothing else. MC wants to be angry about this. He is not angry about it.

## 9. Vulnerability

**Load-bearing, singular: he cannot speak his own name aloud inside the dungeon.**

Not "will not." *Cannot.* The words do not arrive in his mouth. The lease does not contain his name as data — it contains his name as *signature*, which is a different structure. A signature is a gesture, not a word. If he tries to say it, his mouth forms around the sounds and then the sounds do not come. He can write it (he does not). He can nod to it (he has never been asked). He cannot speak it.

**Why this is the real vulnerability:** because the dungeon's contradiction is solved in the 天井 by speaking truth in the right register. If MC ever needed to save him by naming him in the 天井, MC would have to name him first — and MC does not know the name. The LI cannot supply it. The vulnerability is not that MC might hurt him; it is that MC might *need* to help him and find that the mechanism of helping requires a word the LI cannot give.

**What happens if a runner violates it** (tries to force the name — interrogates, threatens, demands): the LI's body does not fight. His shadow does. The door-frame on the wall *sharpens* — the carved lintel resolves too clearly, the door-panels visible now with hinges, a keyhole. He becomes more door. The man in the room loses vocabulary; the man's hands go cool and slightly clumsy; his eyes focus later. A sustained pressure can push him back toward the door-state he was in in 2013 — less sitting, less bending, less tenderness. It is the horror version of the cultivation arc running in reverse.

In Ch1 this is not triggered on-page. It is seeded: one moment late in the demo, MC asks the forbidden question ("what's your—") and the shadow sharpens for a half-second. The LI says nothing. MC understands without being told that the question has a cost and stops asking.

## 10. Sexual register

He is an adult. He has been with runners before, in previous dungeon-nights — four of them across thirteen years, each a full intimacy, each a braid not a weld. He holds those memories the way a man holds letters from people who moved abroad: clearly, fondly, without confusion about where those people are now. None of them were in love with him. He was not in love with them. The intimacy was real and bounded.

MC is the first runner whose anchor, under intimacy, goes toward weld instead of braid. He knows this by the second kiss. He does not tell MC.

**What intimacy means for him specifically (vs for the anchor mechanic):** the mechanic is that intimacy stabilizes. For him, sex is the only setting in which his body behaves like a body instead of a proxy. He does not breathe fog; but in the heat of skin, his exhale warms slightly and MC sees, once, a thin plume against his own collarbone. That plume is him the closest he ever gets to alive. He knows what it means. He does not say what it means.

**What his body reveals that his voice will not:**
- He does not initiate. Not because he doesn't want to — because he does not know if his wanting is the man's wanting or the lease's wanting, and he will not impose the confusion on MC. MC initiates every escalation. He responds, fully, with his hands first, his voice last.
- When MC's hands are on him, the knuckle-shadow darkens. The "newsprint bleeding through" tell becomes more visible. MC may or may not notice. If MC asks — and MC won't, not mid-scene — the LI would say "it happens." That's all.
- He makes sound. Low, surprised, not performative. The sound of someone who is being touched with attention for the first time in a long time. (It is not his first time in thirteen years; it is his first time with MC. Both are true.)
- His skin warms only where MC's skin touches it. The rest stays cool. Undressed in the 亭子间 lamplight, he is mapped by the places MC has been.
- Verbal consent beats on page: real, clinical, specific. "Yes. — Yes, there. — Slow." He uses the word *yes* the way a man uses it when he has been waiting to be asked a question. Not urgent. Precise.
- After: he does not sleep. He rests. He watches MC sleep. Not possessively. The way a man watches weather through a window — grateful for it, knowing it will pass, knowing he is not weather.

**What the scene is, for him:** the closest he will come in Ch1 to the ritual of asking the question. The braid does not give him the answer. But the warmth-where-touched, the fog of his one exhale, the sound he made — these are data he has never gathered before. After this night he knows more about the question than he knew at dusk. He does not know the answer. He knows the question has a shape, and the shape is MC.

## 11. What he knows about MC that MC doesn't know he knows

Three observations, each usable as a one-line gift inside his dialogue:

1. **MC stopped calling his sister three months before the dungeon pulled him in.** The LI knows this because MC, twice, has reached for his phone on reflex (a gesture not useful in the 副本), looked at the dark screen, and put it away without scrolling. The second time, his thumb hovered over a specific contact. The LI cannot read the name, but he can see the grief-shape of a person unanswered. Usable line: *"Whoever you were going to call — they will still be there when you get out. Call them then. Or don't. But don't make the silence any longer than it already is."*

2. **MC has been eating alone for at least a year.** Revealed by how MC holds a bowl in the 灶披间 — close to his chest, angled away, the posture of someone who has eaten standing up over a sink. The LI says nothing about it for most of the night. Then, near dawn, while setting a second bowl on the 亭子间 table across from MC's, he positions both bowls exactly equidistant from the table's edge — a small, deliberate correction of a habit MC has not named. Usable line (optional): *"There. Now the table knows it is being eaten at."*

3. **MC has already been disappearing from his own life before the dream got him** — he is the category of person a forgetting-place can mistake for a resident because he was already half-mistaken for one in the waking city. The LI recognized this when MC crossed the 门槛 and did not flinch. People who are fully in their lives flinch at thresholds. The LI does not name this. It is why he is careful with MC in a way he has not been careful with other runners. Usable line, deflected through metaphor: *"You crossed in as if you had been here. That's not the dream's doing. That's what you brought."*

## 12. The cliffhanger around his name

**The scene, last beat of Ch1:** dawn. The 天井 rainwater has shifted from reflecting lanterns to reflecting sky. The 石库门 lintel is humming. MC's anchor has held. They are at the inside of the 石库门, the door about to open. MC turns — not to kiss him, not to touch him; they have already done those things and will do them again. MC turns because the single thing he has not done is say his name. He opens his mouth to say it. And nothing comes.

He does not know it.

He did not learn it. He did not *ask.* Across a whole night of extraordinary intimacy, across the ritual-question scene, across dawn — he never asked. The LI let him not ask, because the LI could not have given the answer even if asked, and because the LI wanted one runner to leave the dungeon without carrying that specific debt.

**How it lands:** MC realizes independently, mid-inhale. His face does the work — not a dramatic beat, a small one, the kind of realization a man has in a kitchen. He looks at the LI. The LI looks back. The LI knows, and MC knows he knows.

**Per world bible §7:** MC cannot ask directly without cost. He does not ask. He says, instead, what is true:

MC (aloud, not a question): "I don't know what to call you."

Beat.

**The LI's last line in Ch1:** *"That's all right. You will next time."*

— delivered evenly, the downward Shanghainese lilt under it. Not a promise. Not a joke. A sentence offered across a threshold that is closing. MC steps through the 石库门 into the real lane, real hour, cold real air. The door closes behind him. He does not turn around. If he turned around the door would not be there. He knows this without knowing how he knows.

**What the line does:** (a) confirms there will be a next time — sequel hook, clean; (b) withholds the name without drawing attention to the withholding; (c) gives MC a gift (reassurance) and keeps the debt (the name) with the LI; (d) is the line that will be stitched on every piece of fan merch and will carry the Demo 2 marketing campaign alone.

## 13. Visual style notes for art

**Grammar.** CN+KR hybrid anime per research §6. Lean-tall KR manhwa silhouette; CN-drama color discipline (muted, materially-literate, no neon). Not painterly (that's JP Nitro-tier). Not flat-chibi (that's CN romance-novel cover). The target is **muted-manhwa with one stone color pulled forward.**

**Skin palette.** Cool ivory base (HSL roughly 30°, 12% sat, 85% light). Never warm-bronze. Shadow temperature cool blue-gray. Highlights never push warm — he is lit by lanterns but his skin does not warm under them. This is a readable wrongness.

**Hair palette.** Near-black with cool blue undertone (roughly HSL 220°, 15% sat, 12% light), never warm black or espresso. Hair always slightly sharper-rendered than his skin — one tonal step more resolved — as if the hair is closer to real than the body is.

**Eye palette.** Dark brown pupils (roughly HSL 25°, 35% sat, 20% light) on a faintly cool white-of-eye. No red. No gold. No supernatural hue. The wrongness in his gaze is *timing*, not color.

**The one distinctive color fans will pull out:** **the gray of his sweater.** A specific dove-gray that sits between cool-charcoal and warm-stone, unsaturated, understated. On Pinterest and XHS this will become "that gray." It is the only color he consistently wears and the only color that is his. Fan art, cosplay, key art, merch — lock this gray, name it internally, share the hex with any art contractor. (Suggested: #A8A9A1, or near it — Regina's art team finalizes.)

**Clothing palette.** Late-20th-century Shanghai residential, quality-fabric-gone-slightly-threadbare. Grays, muted navies, ivory undershirts, soft charcoal trousers, pale-worn leather belts. Nothing chromatic. No red. No gold. No patterns — a man who bought solids. **The palette itself is a character brief: it should feel like a wardrobe owned for decades and cared for.**

**Pose language (critical for standing CGs).**
- Resting stance: weight on left leg slightly more than right. Hands often in trouser pockets — specifically the front pockets, thumbs out, a 1990s-men's-casual posture, not a modern-defensive-closed one. Shoulders relaxed downward. Head angled maybe 3° down, not aggressive, not shy.
- Walking: steady, unhurried, short stride for his height. Never strides. He has lived in this block; he walks like someone who has nowhere to be that he isn't.
- Sitting: when he sits, he sits fully back in the chair. Never on the edge. This is tenant-posture. Runners sit on edges because they are ready to leave. He isn't.
- Standing in the 天井: he stands a specific distance from the walls — an arm's length, always. This is his 执念 tell made spatial. Art team: if in doubt about composition, place him exactly an arm's length from whatever architectural edge frames the shot.
- The shadow: every CG that includes his shadow must render the door-frame outline correctly. This is non-negotiable. If the shadow would break composition, hide the shadow (§3 rules on light angle cover this). Never compromise the shape.

**One reference anchor I want the art team told, verbatim:** *his body is a thin paper laid over a door. Render the paper first. Let the door show at the joints.*

## 14. Archetype anchor + differentiation

**Closest archetype:** **沈巍 (Shen Wei) from 《镇魂》.** Modern-urban CN supernatural, passing-as-human registers, devoted-gong who has waited a very long time, mild-mannered surface over something older and stranger.

**The one specific thing that makes him not Shen Wei:** Shen Wei is a **being** with a biography — a 10,000-year-old 鬼王 who chose the identity of a professor. The LI here is **not a being.** He is a **contract** — a legal-administrative object given human shape, who does not know whether he is the man who signed it or only the signature that outlived him. Shen Wei has an interior answer to the question "what am I?" — he is a 鬼王 acting as a professor. This LI does not have an interior answer. The question is not rhetorical for him; it is the wound, unanswered, and the romance is the attempt to answer it.

**Secondary differentiators (layered below the primary):** not passing-as-human all the time (the shadow is always there, visible or hidden by light); not omniscient about his own nature (Shen Wei knows; this LI is the one least-informed person in the room about himself); not positioned as protector-above-human (he is not above MC, he is symbiotically contingent on being known by a runner, including MC).

Hua Cheng rejected as comp: Hua Cheng chooses devotion across centuries toward a specific divine figure; the LI's devotion is toward the question of his own being, and MC is the vessel through which that question might be answered. Different vector. Wei Wuxian rejected as comp: corpse-cultivation, flute, explicit taxonomy — none present here. The word "ghost" does not apply to this LI.

## 15. Hard guardrails (confirmed)

- Not Hua Cheng. Not Shen Wei. Not Wei Wuxian. No red eyes. No gold eyes. No long black flashy robes. No corpse-binding flute. No silver butterflies. No dice.
- Not named in Ch1. Not in dialogue, not in prose, not in file metadata, not in promotional copy.
- Does not use his real name even in his own internal (off-page) narration. The name exists as signature, not speech.
- Does not explain his nature. He shows; he implies; he deflects when asked directly; he absorbs cost when forced.
- Does not initiate intimacy from a position of power-seeking. MC initiates every escalation. Vulnerability is the whole point.
- Does not call MC pet names in an inaccessible-language way. No 老公. No 心肝. One "my love" across the whole demo, dropped quietly near dawn, in English. Once.
- No Western-demon framing. No "fallen from grace." No halo-of-darkness. No hellfire. His supernatural is **administrative-grief** — the wrongness of a contract given a body, the sorrow of paperwork that outlasted its purpose. This is the register.
