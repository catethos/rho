# W4 — Scene 1 (EN): "The weekday that wasn't"

**Project:** PG18 BL VN Demo 1 — Shanghai shikumen 副本
**Scene:** 1 of 13. Pre-子时, ~22:00–22:45 Thursday, April 17.
**POV:** MC (林知行 / Lin Zhixing), first-person, present-tense leaning.
**Register:** §4 voice at full resolution — pre-dungeon, no degradation.
**Target:** ~1500 words EN.

---

[BG: Long Ning apartment, kitchen-and-living-room, 22:00 Thursday, overhead LED on low]

The laptop lid closes at 22:04 and the room gets fractionally darker, which — statistically — it should not. The overhead is still on. The standing lamp is still on. A laptop screen at 40% brightness cannot account for a perceptible shift in ambient light. And yet. I notice it the way I notice most things in this apartment: after the fact, with a statistician's slight embarrassment at having bothered to notice.

[ACTION: MC stands from the desk]

I stand. My left knee makes the small apologetic sound it has been making since last Tuesday. Forty square meters of apartment unfolds around me like a spreadsheet I already know the totals of. Bedroom through the half-open door. Living room floor, which is also my office floor. Kitchen, which is the counter I am walking toward now. I do not turn on the kitchen light because the spill from the living room is sufficient for reheating, and additionally because turning on a second light feels like an overcommitment.

The *饿了么* bag is in the fridge. I ordered it on Sunday. It is Thursday.

[ACTION: MC opens fridge, retrieves takeout bag]
[SFX: fridge seal releasing, plastic bag crinkle]

*黄焖鸡米饭* — braised chicken over rice, the takeout I order when I am not going to enjoy eating. There is a hierarchy of *饿了么* orders from the office, and this one sits exactly at the fuel-intake floor: hot, sufficient, structurally incapable of disappointing me because it was not asked to do more than exist. I peel the sauce container open. Four days in a fridge and the sauce has separated into its component grievances. I pour it over the rice anyway. The microwave is a Midea from 2021 that makes a plastic ding I have come to think of as punctuation.

[SFX: microwave running, then ding]
[BG: kitchen counter, overhead spill only, night window]
[CG PRIORITY: optional — MC at counter, cardigan not yet on, ring visible on left hand]

I eat standing at the counter. This is not a decision. The small round table — bought in a fit of civic optimism in 2021 — is two and a half meters away and currently holding a stack of last quarter's printouts I have been meaning to shred. I could move them. I could sit. I eat standing. The rice is fine. The chicken has the particular post-fridge texture of something that was never going to be anything, reheated into the fact of it.

Outside the kitchen window the pothos is dead. *绿萝*. Has been since Chinese New Year. Two months and change of being decorative in a new way — the stems have dried into a rust-colored spiral that reaches toward the glass like it remembered, at the last minute, where the light used to be. I catalogue it each time I do dishes. I do not throw it out. Throwing it out would be a decision, and the plant is not asking me to make one.

[ACTION: MC sets down chopsticks, runs thumb across band of ring on left index finger]

My thumb finds the ring without being told. Silver, plain, the kind of thing you can buy on *淘宝* for eighty *kuai* that my sister somehow found in 2019 for — I checked the box, later, I am that kind of person — two hundred and forty. *Graduation gift, Jr-sshing. Now you have to go be a serious person.* I was 22. I was not going to be a serious person. I was going to be a person who had a job. The ring did not know the difference. Seven years on the left index finger. I have taken it off twice — once in 2021 for an MRI, once in 2023 because I thought it was scratching a keyboard I was about to sell. Both times I put it back on within the hour. It is the only object on my body that does not feel like administrative overhead.

[BG: counter, half-eaten takeout, phone face-down]

The phone buzzes. Not a call. A *微信* notification — work group, Zhou-laoshi asking if someone can check Q3 attrition numbers before the Monday deck. Not my line item. I lower the phone face-down and the movement is what reminds me.

Thirteen days.

[ACTION: MC turns phone face-up, opens call log]

*林知雅 · 4月4日 · 未接来电 · 语音留言 · 未播放.*

My sister's contact photo is still the one from her wedding last April. She is mid-laugh. Her husband is at the edge of the frame, half-turned, which is what you get when you screenshot someone else's Moments. I have looked at this photograph more than I have spoken to the woman in it, this year. The voicemail icon is a small grey waveform. Twenty-two seconds. I could play it in twenty-two seconds. I could play it while I finish eating. I could play it and then put the phone down, and the difference between the minute before and the minute after would be containable.

[ACTION: MC turns phone face-down. Picks up chopsticks.]

I do not play it. I turn the phone face-down, which is — I notice — the gesture you make to a card you are not ready to read. Fine. That is one metaphor I will allow myself for the evening.

[ACTION: MC's eyes cross the bookshelf while chewing]

There are two IKEA shelves between the kitchen and the living room. The top shelf is finance and stats — Damodaran, two Hull editions, Wasserman's *All of Statistics* which I still reach for when I am tired. Between Damodaran and the 2015 Hull, tucked sideways so the spine doesn't show, is a small framed photograph I have never upgraded.

*Nai-nai. 1946–2022.*

My eyes cross the frame, register the shape of it, and move on. Rice. Chopsticks. The specific way the *绿萝* stem curves at the window.

Fine.

[ACTION: MC finishes eating, rinses bowl, sets it in the rack]

I put the bowl in the rack instead of the dishwasher because the machine is only a third full and running it for one bowl offends the part of me that was raised on a pharmaceutical distributor's salary. Water still on my wrist. I dry my hands on the dishtowel, which has not been changed this week.

The phone — face-down — buzzes again. This one I check.

*周老师 · 19:42 · 「知行, 不好意思这么晚. 那个硬盘明天你方不方便顺路送到我家? 西康路xxx弄x号亭子间. 明早我要直接去机场. 🙏 我已经睡了, 你自己摸上楼放门口就好.」*

[INTERIOR: MC reads the message twice]

*The emoji is the tell.* Zhou-laoshi is 41, has two kids, deploys 🙏 only when he is past the point of additional politeness — the one-character apology that is also a whole conversation he doesn't have time for. *西康路*. Xuhui / Jing'an border. I checked the address against the office directory last week when he first mentioned the drive — a *亭子间*, which surprised me; I had assumed he lived in a newer building with his wife's family. Some people keep a separate address for work things. I did not ask.

The hard drive is in my bag. I packed it this morning. I have been packing it every morning this week, which is more days than it should have taken to deliver it.

[ACTION: MC checks wrist — 22:31]

22:31. Line 2 runs until 23:00. *高德地图* suggests 28 minutes via 2号线 transfer to 7号线, plus eight minutes on foot at the far end. I could go tomorrow morning. Zhou-laoshi said to come up quietly and leave it at the door; clearly he assumes tomorrow. *已经睡了*. Zhou-laoshi's phrasing is always precise: *I am already asleep* — not *I will be asleep* — which is the kind of sentence you write to a person you are trusting not to wake you.

I could go tomorrow morning.

[ACTION: MC puts on the grey cardigan, picks up the laptop bag with the drive]

I put on the cardigan. The grey one. I pick up the bag. I leave.

[ACTION: MC in doorway, pats pockets — wallet, keys, phone]
[SFX: apartment door closing, mechanical lock, elevator descent]

Outside it is twenty degrees at 22:37 on the seventeenth of April, which even *上海天气* has been calling *偏暖* for a week. The air on my wrist, where the cardigan cuff rides up, is the temperature of a person's hand. A motorcycle is idling at the corner of 长宁路 — the delivery driver is lifting the lid on his insulated box, checking an order under the streetlight, and for a half-second his whole attention is on the small labeled bag with an expression more careful than any expression I have brought to my own life this week. I like him instantly. I like most strangers instantly. It is the shortest distance between me and a person I cannot disappoint.

I walk toward the subway. I am not in a hurry. The metro entrance is two hundred and forty meters from my building — I know because I counted once, in 2022, for reasons I no longer recall. The warm air moves around my cardigan the way warm air does when no one is paying attention to it, and I am, for this specific stretch of pavement, inside the smallest possible version of my life: a 28-year-old man delivering a hard drive to a coworker who is already asleep, because that is what was asked of him, and the asking was small, and he is the kind of person who does the small things when asked.

I do not know yet that *西康路* is not only a street.

[SCENE ENDS — pre-threshold. Clean cut before Scene 2.]
