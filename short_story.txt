# The Lighthouse Keeper's Last Letter

The lighthouse had been abandoned for thirty years when Clara found the bottle.

She had been exploring the rocky coast of Maine, searching for sea glass after the storm, when something glinted in the sand near the old lighthouse's foundation. Buried beneath a century of sand and salt, she discovered a glass bottle—thick, green, and sealed with wax.

Inside was a letter.

---

**Dear Whoever Finds This,**

My name is Samuel, and I have been the keeper of the Merrow Point Lighthouse for forty-seven years. Tonight, a terrible storm approaches, and I am old. My hands shake. My eyes fail me. I know I will not survive what comes.

But I must tell someone what I have seen.

For three decades, I have watched the waters. Not just for ships—there is something else beneath the surface. Something ancient. On nights when the moon is dark, the water glows. And shapes move beneath. Not fish. Not whales. Something that remembers when the first ships came to these shores.

I have been their witness. And they have let me live, I think, because I never interfered. I simply kept the light burning, guiding ships away from the rocks—and away from them.

Tonight, I will not light the flame.

They have called to me. Finally, they wish to meet the man who has watched them for so long. I am afraid. But I am also ready.

If you are reading this, the lighthouse has fallen silent. Please do not blame the coast guard for what happens next. They cannot help. No one can.

But perhaps, if you are brave, you can learn what I could not. Perhaps you can be the bridge between their world and ours.

The lamp is in the study, second drawer. The key is beneath the mat.

Good luck.

—Samuel
Merrow Point Keeper, 1893

---

Clara read the letter three times. The wind howled behind her, and the lighthouse towered above, dark and empty.

She looked toward the sea. The water was calm—too calm. And beneath the surface, a faint green glow pulsed like a heartbeat.

The lamp was waiting.

With trembling hands, Clara walked toward the lighthouse door.

---

*The Merrow Point Lighthouse was never found by the coast guard. But locals say that on certain nights, when the moon is dark, a faint light still flickers from the tower. And sometimes, if you listen closely to the waves, you can hear something that sounds almost like singing.*
